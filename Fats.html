Fats

Fats are a type of nutrient found in food that the body needs to stay healthy. They provide a major source of energy and help the body store energy for later use. Fats are also important for protecting organs, keeping the body warm, and helping the body absorb certain vitamins such as vitamins A, D, E, and K. Although fats are essential, they should be eaten in balanced amounts, with healthier fats being better for the body than unhealthy ones.

The Benefits of Fats

Fats are an important part of a healthy diet because they provide energy and help the body function properly. They support cell growth and protect organs by acting as insulation. Fats also help the body absorb essential vitamins such as vitamins A, D, E, and K, which are necessary for good vision, strong bones, and a healthy immune system. In addition, healthy fats, like those found in fish, nuts, and olive oil, support brain function and heart health when eaten in the right amounts.

The Food Source of Fats

1.Fatty Fish

Fatty fish are rich in unsaturated fats and omega-3 fatty acids, which are very important for heart and brain health. The American Heart Association recommends eating two servings of fatty fish each week, with one serving being about 3 ounces of cooked fish. Examples of fatty fish include tuna, herring, mackerel, salmon, sardines, and anchovies. Both fresh and canned fish are good sources of omega-3 fatty acids, making them a convenient and healthy choice. Canned fish such as tuna or mackerel is especially helpful for people who have limited access to fresh food, as it still provides enough omega-3 to support a healthy diet.

2.Dark chocolate

A 28.35 g (1 oz) serving of dark chocolate with 70% to 85% cocoa contains about 12.1 g of fat and provides nutrients such as potassium, calcium, and magnesium. However, a large part of this fat is saturated fat, so it should be eaten in limited amounts. Dark chocolate also contains some added sugar, which means it is best consumed in moderation. Even so, dark chocolate has flavonoid antioxidants that can support brain and heart health.

3.Eggs

Eggs are a popular and nutritious food, especially for vegetarians. One 50 g hard-boiled egg contains about 5.3 g of fat, including a small amount of saturated fat, and provides around 78 calories. Egg yolks are rich in vitamin D and choline, which support the brain, liver, nerves, and muscles. They also contain helpful nutrients like lutein. Studies have shown that eating less than one egg per day may help lower the risk of cardiovascular disease.

4.Avocado

A 100 g serving of avocado contains about 14.7 g of fat and 160 calories. Most of this fat is oleic acid, a healthy monounsaturated fat that may help reduce inflammation and support overall health. Avocados are also high in fiber, providing about 6.7 g per 100 g, which helps digestion. In addition, they contain lutein for eye health and are a good source of potassium, which supports proper muscle and heart function.
