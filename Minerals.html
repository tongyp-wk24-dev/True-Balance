Minerals

Mineral Nutrition is defined as the naturally occurring inorganic nutrient found in the soil and food that is essential for the proper functioning of animal and plant body. Minerals are vital elements necessary for the body. Both the plants and animals require minerals essentially. For example, Zinc is necessary for the manufacture of protein and for cell division.

The Benefits of Minerals

Some of the benefits include maintaining bone health, reducing muscular dystrophy- muscles weakness, promoting strong bones, maintaining blood vessels function, promoting dental health, improving brain function, ensuring blood circulation, and much more.

The Food Source of Vitamins

1.Nuts and seeds

Nuts and seeds are highly nutritious foods that are rich in many important minerals, especially magnesium, zinc, manganese, copper, selenium, and phosphorus. Some types are particularly high in certain minerals, such as Brazil nuts, where just one nut can provide more than the daily requirement of selenium, and pumpkin seeds, which offer a large amount of magnesium in a small serving. Whole nuts and seeds make a healthy and convenient snack, while nut and seed butters can be easily added to smoothies, oatmeal, or eaten with fruits and vegetables for extra nutrition.

2.Shellfish

Shellfish, such as oysters, clams, and mussels, are rich in important minerals like selenium, zinc, copper, and iron. Eating just six medium-sized oysters (84 grams) can meet daily needs for zinc and copper and provide significant amounts of selenium and iron. Zinc is essential for immune function, DNA production, cell division, and protein production. Certain groups, including pregnant and breastfeeding women, people with digestive issues, those on some medications, adolescents, and older adults, may be at risk of zinc deficiency, which can weaken the immune system and affect growth. Shellfish are a concentrated source of zinc, making them a smart choice for those needing this nutrient.

3.Cruciferous vegetables

Eating cruciferous vegetables, such as cauliflower, broccoli, Swiss chard, and Brussels sprouts, offers many health benefits, including reducing the risk of chronic diseases. These benefits come from their high nutrient content, especially minerals. Cruciferous vegetables like broccoli, kale, cabbage, and watercress are rich in sulfur, which is important for cell function, DNA production, detoxification, and the creation of glutathione, a powerful antioxidant. They also provide other essential minerals, including magnesium, potassium, manganese, and calcium, making them a highly nutritious addition to any diet.

4.Organ Meats

Organ meats, though less commonly eaten than chicken or steak, are some of the most mineral-rich foods available. For example, an 85-gram slice of beef liver meets your daily copper needs and provides significant amounts of selenium, zinc, iron, and phosphorus. In addition to minerals, organ meats are also high in protein and important vitamins, including vitamin B12, vitamin A, and folate, making them a highly nutritious food choice.
